🔒 Dive into the exciting world of cybersecurity! Below, I have curated a selection of online platforms and resources that have helped me learn and practice my cybersecurity skills. I challenge myself daily to improve my abilities in a safe and controlled environment.

- [Hack The Box](https://www.hackthebox.eu) 🎯: An online penetration platform that offers virtual machines for users to explore and find vulnerabilities.

- [TryHackMe](https://tryhackme.com) 🏰: An online platform that provides a wide range of challenges and cybersecurity labs.

- [OverTheWire](https://overthewire.org/wargames/) 🔐: A series of online challenges designed to help beginners learn and practice cybersecurity skills.

- [VulnHub](https://www.vulnhub.com) 🏢: A platform that hosts vulnerable virtual machines for users to practice their hacking and security skills.

- [PentesterLab](https://pentesterlab.com) 🛠️: An online training platform that offers labs and practical exercises to learn about cybersecurity and penetration testing.

- [Portswigger Web Security Academy](https://portswigger.net/web-security) 🕷️: Interactive labs and exercises to learn web application security techniques.

- [Blue Team Lab Defenders](https://blueteamlabs.online/) 🛡️: Practice and learn defensive cybersecurity skills in simulated environments.

- [Root-Me](https://www.root-me.org) 🌱: An online security platform that provides challenges and labs to learn about hacking and security.

- [CTF365](https://ctf365.com) 🚩: An online platform for training and competition based on cybersecurity challenges.

- [Hacker101](https://www.hacker101.com) 🎓: An online educational platform that offers a variety of resources and security challenges.

- [Hacking-Lab](https://www.hacking-lab.com) 🏢: An online platform that provides cybersecurity labs and challenges to learn and practice ethical hacking skills.

- [OWASP WebGoat](https://www.owasp.org/index.php/OWASP_WebGoat_Project) 🐐: A deliberately insecure web application used to practice and learn about common vulnerabilities in web applications.

- [Damn Vulnerable Web Application (DVWA)](https://dvwa.co.uk) 🎯: A deliberately insecure web application used to learn and practice security techniques.

- [OWASP Juice Shop](https://owasp.org/www-project-juice-shop/) 🍊: A purposely insecure web application developed to practice and learn about web application security.

- [HackThisSite](https://www.hackthissite.org) 💻: A platform that provides online security challenges and exercises to practice ethical hacking skills.

- [CrackMe](https://crackmes.one) 🔓: A collection of reverse engineering and software cracking challenges to practice analysis and manipulation skills.

- [RingZer0 Team Online CTF](https://ringzer0ctf.com) 🎮: A platform that hosts online CTF competitions and challenges to practice cybersecurity skills.

- [MicroCorruption](https://microcorruption.com) 🔍: A reverse engineering challenge based on a microcontroller to learn and practice firmware analysis skills.

- [PicoCTF](https://picoctf.org) 🏆: An online CTF competition aimed at beginners and students, offering security challenges of varying difficulty levels.

- [Exploit Exercises](https://exploit-exercises.com) 💣: A series of challenges and labs to practice exploitation and penetration testing skills.

I encourage you to explore these pages and enjoy the experience of practicing cybersecurity! Always remember to conduct your activities ethically and legally, respecting the privacy and rights of others. Let's have fun and keep learning together!
